# Variance
In [[Probability Theory\|Probability Theory]], the [[01-atomic/math/Variance\|Variance]] is the [[01-atomic/math/Expected Value\|Expected Value]] of the squared deviation from the mean of a [[Random Variable\|Random Variable]].

Variance is a measure of dispersion, meaning it's measure how far a set of numbers are spread out from the average value. It's also the second [[Central Moment\|Central Moment]] of a [[Probability Distribution\|Probability Distribution]].
## Formulas

> [!formula] Variance Definition of a Random Variable
> For a [[Random Variable\|Random Variable]], it's variance it's defined by it's [[01-atomic/math/Expected Value\|Expected Value]] and the square deviation from the mean $(\mu)$
> $$Var(X) = E[(X - \mu)^2]$$

This definition encompasses random variables that are generated by processes that are [[Continuous Probability Distribution\|Continuous Probability Distribution]], [[Discrete Probability Distribution\|Discrete Probability Distribution]], or mixed.

> [!formula] Variance of a Random Variable with Covariance
> For a [[Random Variable\|Random Variable]] $X$, the Variance can be calculated as the [[Covariance\|Covariance]] of the random variable with itself
> #formula $$Var(X) = Cov(X,X)$$

> [!formula] Expansion of the Definition for the Variance
> The Variance definition for a [[Random Variable\|Random Variable]] $X$ can also be expressed as:
> $$Var(X) = E[(X - E[X])^2]$$
> which is equivalent to:
> #formula $$Var(X) = E[X^2] - E[X]^2$$

> The last equation should not be used in [[Floating Point Arithmetic\|Floating Point Arithmetic]], as it has [[Loss Significance\|Loss Significance]]
